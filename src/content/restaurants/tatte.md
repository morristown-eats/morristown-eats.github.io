*Tatte is a regional chain out of Boston with locations across the Northeast. Under Morristown Eats editorial policy, regional chains are covered for context and community value — not for awards eligibility. Coverage is honest about what it is.*

---

Tatte is a chain. Boston origin, now spread across New Jersey, DC, Maryland, New York. That's worth saying upfront.

It's also genuinely good at what it does, which is why it gets a mention here.

The space is large and light — first-come seating, lots of it, which makes it easy on weekends when you have kids and no appetite for waiting. The outdoor seating is the move in summer. On weekdays the crowd shifts to Sanofi workers from the building above taking breaks between meetings — a different kind of packed, a different energy. Both versions work.

The pistachio latte is the thing to get. It's the reason people line up. We shared one while waiting for Morris Proper to open next door, which is exactly the right use for it: something to hold while you're somewhere else.

For brunch it's a solid option when you don't want to plan ahead, want space for kids to breathe, and would rather spend fifteen minutes in line than forty-five waiting for a table somewhere more ambitious. It earns its spot.
