The Morris Proper sits at the base of The Morris hotel on Speedwell, floor-to-ceiling glass looking out at the roundabout that funnels traffic up toward the Green. At 11am on a Friday it's empty and airy — big room, bar anchored in the center, open kitchen visible to the side. It feels like a good hotel lobby in a city where the hotel actually cares about what it's serving.

Hotel restaurants earn their skepticism because most of them don't have to try. This is our third or fourth visit, and it tries every time.

Start with the focaccia ($14) — whipped ricotta, market conserva, olive relish — and add the local prosciutto ($8). The smoked-then-fried wings ($15) are the kind of bar food that has clearly been thought about: lime cabbage slaw, peanuts, chili fish sauce, wings seasoned by someone who wanted you to pay attention. Both are worth ordering before anything else.

The crab and jalapeño pasta ($33) uses housemade linguine and it shows. The pasta itself is cooked right, which sounds like a baseline — but at a hotel restaurant it's worth noting when it actually happens. The cocktail program is where the bar earns its place in the center of the room. The Paper Plane ($17, Michter's bourbon, Aperol, lemon) is delicious and springy. The Sweater Weather ($18, Casamigos reposado, blood orange, Cointreau, ginger) is better and bigger — if you're ordering one, order that.

The thing nobody talks about: lunch. The kitchen runs a double smashburger that is one of the more quietly excellent burgers in Morristown — elevated without being fussy, worth coming for on its own. Overheard the line calling it "burger hour." Good instinct.

No reservation needed for a weekday lunch. Show up, sit anywhere, take the room in. You're not in a hotel anymore, you're just somewhere good.
